SPAN 350 - Final Presentation Guidelines 
 
 
 
General instructions: The final presentation will consist of a 10-minute 
presentation in English to be completed in pairs (5 minutes per presenter) 
about one of the topics discussed in class (or another topic related to the 
course) about which students will investigate on the internet and/or in the 
library. As modeled by the class, the discussion related to these topics 
should be tied to a specific Spanish-speaking country. Presentation content 
will mainly focus on culture but should include connections with 
Spanish-language concepts.  
 
Potential topics are listed below (though additional topics will also be 
considered): 
 
-Idiomatic expressions in a Spanish-speaking country (Presenters 
must consider how these products relate to perspectives and 
practices in the region).  
-Machismo 
-Marianismo  
-The use of gestures in a particular Spanish-speaking country 
-Inclusive language 
-The role of sports in a Spanish-speaking country 
-University life in a specific Spanish-speaking country 
-Spanglish 
-The analysis of a Latin American or Spanish literary or artistic work 
(Presenters must consider how this product relates to perspectives 
and practices in its corresponding Spanish-Speaking country). 
 
Additional instructions:  
-The presentation topic must be approved by your instructor prior to Week 
15. Projects not approved by the instructor will automatically be deducted 
10% of the grade.  
 
-The presentation should be enhanced by a PowerPoint presentation and 
must be memorized and not read.  
 
-PowerPoint presentations must be submitted by November 30th via iLearn 
and must include a Bibliography page.
